Markdown

[A] way to style text on the web. You control the display of the document; formatting words as bold or italic, adding images, and creating lists are just a few of the things we can do with Markdown. Mostly, Markdown is just regular text with a few non-alphabetic characters thrown in, like # or *.

Headers

Use a "#" to change header size

# Best Chocolate Chip Cookies

## Best Chocolate Chip Cookies

### Best Chocolate Chip Cookies

#### Best Chocolate Chip Cookies

##### Best Chocolate Chip Cookies
###### Best Chocolate Chip Cookies

Bold text

Surround text with "**" to bold it

Bake for **10 minutes** or until golden brown.

Italics

In Markdown, any text that you would like to be in italics should be surrounded by single underscores.

The secret ingredient is the coconut! _Be warned_, these will fly off of the plate!

Lists and Unordered lists

Use an "*" for unordered lists.

Use a numbers followed by a period for ordered lists.

  • *Butter
  • *White Sugar
  • *Brown Sugar
  • *Eggs
  1. First step
  2. Second step
  3. Third step

Links and Images

In Markdown, a link is composed of two elements, the text that will display on the page surrounded by [] and the web address of where the link will go surrounded by (). Here is an example:


In Markdown, images are added very similarly to links, but the square brackets are preceded by ! and contain alt text for the visually impaired, and the () can contain a web address for an image.

[Click here](http://allrecipes.com/) to check out my other great recipes.
